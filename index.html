Phase 1 · Foundation

Build the Business Case

Establish the evidence base that justifies investment in a Women Centred CVP — grounded in data, market insight and clear segment definition.

Key Activities
📊

Gender Disaggregated Data

Audit internal data (CRM, transactional, NPS) and supplement with external gender-lens datasets to surface the women's opportunity.

🔍

Market Research

Size the total addressable market for women's financial services in target geographies. Benchmark against regional and global peers.

🎯

Target Segment Identification

Define 1–3 priority women's segments by income band, life stage or business size that represent the highest value and strategic fit.

💡

Opportunity Sizing

Translate data into a financial model — revenue uplift, wallet share, retention and NPS gains — to secure executive sponsorship.

📐

Market Sizing

South Africa retail market sizing — TAM, SAM and SOM analysis for women's financial services using Global Findex data.

Key Questions to Answer
What % of our client base is women? What is their revenue contribution vs. potential? Which segments are underserved? What does the competitive landscape look like? What is the ROI of serving women better?

Phase 2 · Design

CVP Development

Translate segment insight into a compelling, differentiated value proposition — solving real pains and amplifying meaningful gains for women clients.

Key Activities
🗣️

Pains, Gains & Jobs to be Done

Run structured focus groups and design thinking workshops (DX) to deeply understand what women need — financially and beyond.

✨

Gain Creators

Design features, services and experiences that directly amplify outcomes women value: wealth building, business growth, security, community.

🩹

Pain Relievers

Remove friction points: complex processes, non-inclusive language, products not designed for women's financial journeys.

🏗️

CVP Architecture

Bundle products, services, digital features and partnerships into a coherent, branded women's proposition with clear articulation.

CVP Building Blocks
Transactional banking Investment & savings SME lending Insurance Financial education Mentorship networks Ecosystem partnerships Digital-first UX

Phase 3 · Planning

Strategy & Go-to-market

Define the full GTM plan — from brand and tone of voice to channel strategy, timelines, budgets and the KPIs that will define success.

Key Activities
🛍️

Products & NPS

Finalise the product set tied to the CVP and establish NPS baselines to measure women's experience uplift post-launch.

🏷️

Brand & Tone of Voice

Develop inclusive, empowering brand positioning and communications guidelines that genuinely resonate with women clients.

📡

Placement & Channels

Map the optimal mix of digital, branch, partner and community channels to reach and engage target segments at every touchpoint.

🗂️

Data Flags & Definition

Establish the data taxonomy to identify, tag and track women clients across systems — enabling segmentation and ongoing measurement.

📅

Timeline

Build the phased launch roadmap with clear milestones: pilot, soft-launch, full rollout and scale — with owners and dependencies.

💰

Budgets & KPIs

Allocate budget across product, marketing and operations. Define the KPI framework — acquisition, retention, revenue, engagement and NPS.

Phase 4 · Alignment

Internal Alignment

Mobilise the organisation — build the governance structure, empower champions and equip every team to deliver the women's CVP with confidence.

Key Activities
📋

Implementation Plan

Translate the strategy into a detailed workplan: who owns what, by when, with clear interdependencies and escalation paths.

👥

Ownership & Champions

Appoint an executive sponsor, program owner, cross-functional working party and branch-level champions to drive accountability.

📣

Internal Comms

Design an internal campaign to build awareness, excitement and commitment across business units — from frontline to C-suite.

🎓

Training Programme

Develop gender-intelligence training for all client-facing staff and equip relationship managers with the tools to have better conversations with women clients.

Success Markers
Executive sponsor confirmed Working party active All staff trained Internal comms launched RACI documented

Phase 5 · Launch

Execution

Launch with impact — run the campaign, roll out the programme and forge the partnerships that extend Nedbank's reach into women's communities.

Key Activities
🚀

Launch Campaign

Execute a multi-channel marketing campaign targeting priority segments — digital, social, branch activation, PR and community events.

📦

Rollout Programme

Phase the rollout by segment and geography. Capture real-time learnings to refine the CVP before each subsequent wave.

🤝

Forge Partnerships

Activate ecosystem partners — women's business networks, NGOs, fintechs, corporates, government — to extend reach and deepen value.

🔁

Feedback Loops

Establish rapid client feedback mechanisms (NPS surveys, focus groups, digital analytics) to enable agile iteration throughout rollout.

Launch Readiness Checklist
Product live in systems Marketing assets approved Staff trained & briefed Partners onboarded KPI dashboard live Comms plan activated

Phase 6 · Sustain

Monitoring & Evaluation

Close the loop — track performance against KPIs, identify what's working, course-correct and continuously raise the bar for women's experience.

Key Activities
📈

Track KPIs

Monitor the full KPI suite: acquisition rates, product penetration, revenue per client, NPS, retention, and engagement metrics — disaggregated by gender.

🔄

Periodic Programme Review

Conduct quarterly programme reviews with the working party. Assess results, surface blockers and agree adjustments to strategy or execution.

📉

Gender Data Scorecards

Publish internal gender data scorecards to track progress against targets and maintain accountability across the organisation.

🌱

Scale & Iterate

Use data to identify the next wave of segments, products and markets to scale into — making the programme self-reinforcing over time.

KPI Framework
New women clients acquired Product cross-sell ratio Women NPS vs. total NPS Revenue from women segment Churn rate Digital engagement SME loan book — women Staff gender-IQ score
